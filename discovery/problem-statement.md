# Problem Statement

## The Problem

Elderly Indian parents who manage household grocery shopping find it difficult to:
- Type grocery lists on small phone keyboards
- Use complex grocery or notes apps with many features
- Communicate their needs to family members in a structured way

Traditional paper lists get lost. Typed lists require smartphone literacy they don't have.

## The Opportunity

These users **can speak naturally in Hindi** and are comfortable with voice commands on phones (e.g., calling someone).

A voice-first app in Hindi removes the literacy barrier and lets them create and manage grocery lists the same way they'd narrate a list out loud.

## Solution

A mobile-first, voice-driven grocery list web app:
- Speaks and listens in Hindi
- Requires zero typing
- Works with or without a Google account
- Displays items in a clean, readable table
- Runs in the browser — no app store download needed

## Success Criteria

- User can create a full grocery list without touching the keyboard
- AI correctly extracts item name, quantity, and brand from natural Hindi speech
- App works reliably on Android Chrome
- Elderly user can navigate the app independently after one demo
